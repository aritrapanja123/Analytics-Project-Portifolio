# Behavioural Operations

## Project Overview

This project explores how behavioural factors influence operational and economic decision-making. It examines the difference between predictions based on purely rational models and decisions observed when fairness, emotion, social norms, and strategic behaviour are considered.

## Business Problem

Traditional decision models often assume that individuals maximise their own utility rationally. Real decisions can differ because people respond to perceived fairness, social expectations, risk, emotion, and the behaviour of others.

## Objectives

- Examine behavioural influences on decision-making.
- Apply Expected Utility Theory.
- Analyse strategic behaviour through the ultimatum game.
- Compare theoretical predictions with observed behavioural responses.
- Consider implications for operational and managerial decisions.

## Analytical Approach

The project evaluates decision behaviour using economic and behavioural concepts. The ultimatum game provides a framework for examining how fairness perceptions can influence offers, acceptance decisions, and outcomes.

Expected Utility Theory is used as a foundation for understanding rational choice under uncertainty, while behavioural observations are used to examine where real decisions may depart from theoretical predictions.

## Business Value

Behavioural understanding can improve the design of:

- incentives;
- negotiation processes;
- customer journeys;
- operational policies;
- workforce processes;
- pricing and offer structures;
- change-management interventions.

## Tools and Techniques

Expected Utility Theory, behavioural analysis, economic reasoning, strategic decision analysis, critical evaluation, and interpretation of behavioural outcomes.

## Skills Demonstrated

Structured reasoning, behavioural decision analysis, critical thinking, theoretical application, business interpretation, and communication of complex decision concepts.

## Future Improvements

The work could be extended using experimental data, behavioural segmentation, A/B testing, prospect theory, bounded rationality models, and applications to digital product or customer journey design.
