# Risk Management and Decision-Making Under Uncertainty

## Project Overview

This project examines how uncertainty can be identified, prioritised, modelled, and incorporated into project planning and decision-making.

## Business Problem

Complex projects face uncertainty in execution, timing, dependencies, resource availability, and stakeholder expectations. Static risk registers may not be sufficient when risks evolve throughout delivery.

## Objectives

- Examine structured approaches to uncertainty management.
- Understand how uncertainty affects project plans.
- Evaluate the role of task breakdown, timing estimates, dependencies, and slack.
- Critically assess the strengths and limitations of risk-management frameworks.
- Connect risk analysis with active decision-making.

## Analytical Approach

The project considers a structured process including:

- uncertainty identification;
- uncertainty selection and prioritisation;
- base-plan modelling;
- task decomposition;
- timing parameters;
- dependency analysis;
- slack analysis;
- network thinking;
- Gantt planning;
- iterative review.

## Key Insight

Risk management is most valuable when it supports decisions rather than functioning only as a compliance exercise. Project uncertainty changes over time, so risk analysis should be connected to planning, monitoring, dependencies, and management action.

## Business Value

The approach can support:

- project planning;
- programme governance;
- dependency management;
- contingency planning;
- prioritisation of management attention;
- decision-making under uncertainty.

## Tools and Techniques

Risk analysis, uncertainty management, project planning, dependency analysis, scenario reasoning, critical evaluation, and decision support.

## Skills Demonstrated

Risk identification, structured uncertainty analysis, project-planning logic, dependency thinking, critical evaluation of frameworks, and management-focused communication.

## Future Improvements

Future work could add quantitative risk simulation, Monte Carlo analysis, probability-impact modelling, sensitivity analysis, and dynamic risk dashboards.
